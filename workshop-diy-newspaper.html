WORKSHOP-DIY DEPLOYS 39TH APPLICATION; DECLARES "BUGS ARE FEATURES" OFFICIAL POLICY

French association's browser-based ecosystem now spans robotics, AI, cybersecurity, and seven total domains ‚Äî WiFi password remains "password123"
By Our Technology Correspondent

In what officials are calling "the most ambitious deployment of educational software by a French association that also teaches children to hack printers ethically," Workshop-DIY has officially released its 39th browser-based application.

The ecosystem, which requires zero installation and runs entirely in web browsers, now covers seven distinct domains: robotics, artificial intelligence, electronics, 3D printing, programming, cybersecurity, and DevOps.

"We started with one app to control a robot via Bluetooth," said the founder, speaking from behind a fortress of coffee cups. "Somehow we ended up with 39 apps and a community of kids who can explain Docker to their parents."

The association, registered under France's Loi 1901 framework, operates on a simple premise: if a child can open a web browser, they can learn to build robots, train AI, and‚Äîperhaps most controversially‚Äîdiscover that the WiFi password is password123.

Inside the Ecosystem

Notable applications include bit-bot, which controls physical robots via Web Bluetooth; magic-hands, a MediaPipe-powered hand-tracking visual effects tool; and pentest-lab, an ethical hacking sandbox that has been credited with at least one school printer vulnerability disclosure.

The ESP32 Robot Kit Lite, developed specifically for workshops, has survived 47 drops from student desks. "The durability testing was entirely involuntary," noted the engineering team.
